Is Lead Generation Worth It for Guelph Contractors?

By Mark Hurson · 2026-06-14 · WeProvideLeads

The honest answer depends on what type of lead generation you’re evaluating. “Lead generation” covers a wide range of products and the quality difference between them is enormous. Here’s a framework for thinking through whether any specific approach is worth the cost for a Guelph-based contractor.

The baseline question: what does a job cost you to acquire?

Before evaluating any lead generation service, calculate your current customer acquisition cost. If you get most of your work through referrals, your acquisition cost is essentially zero — but referrals are lumpy and hard to scale. If you want predictable volume, you need a reliable inbound channel, and that channel has a cost.

The benchmark that matters isn’t “is this lead cheap?” It’s “is my cost to acquire a job lower than what I make on it?” For a roofing job averaging $8,000, a $200/month lead generation cost that produces even one job is obviously worth it. The calculation changes at lower average job values.

What to evaluate before signing up

Verifiable rankings

Any lead generation provider claiming to send you organic (non-ad) leads should be able to show you a real website that actually ranks. Ask them to pull up Google Search Console data or simply Google the trade + city yourself. If the site doesn’t appear on page one for obvious local searches, the organic lead promise is hollow.

At WeProvideLeads, we share ranking data openly — because it’s the whole product. Guelphbasementwaterproofing.com ranks #1 for “basement waterproofing Guelph.” You can verify it in 30 seconds.

Lead quality signals

A lead from a homeowner who searched “mold removal Guelph,” found a dedicated mold removal website, and filled out a form is a fundamentally different thing than a lead from a homeowner who clicked a generic ad and submitted their info to four companies at once. Both are called “leads.” Only one is likely to result in a booked job.

Exclusivity

See our article on exclusive vs. shared leads for a full breakdown. The short version: non-exclusive leads close at 10–20%; exclusive leads from specific service sites close at 40–60%. This difference overwhelms the per-lead cost in almost every calculation.

Contract terms

A lead generation provider confident in their product won’t need a long-term contract. Month-to-month is the right structure — if the leads are good, you keep paying; if they stop delivering, you cancel. Long contracts are often a sign the provider knows the service doesn’t retain customers on merit.

Guelph-specific considerations

Guelph and Wellington County have a population of roughly 175,000 across Guelph proper plus Fergus, Elora, Rockwood, Erin, and Puslinch. It’s large enough to support active search volume for most home service trades, but small enough that dominating local search for a specific trade is achievable. A single well-ranked website like guelphelectrical.com can realistically capture most of the homeowner search traffic for electrical services in the area.

That concentration is actually an advantage for the owned-site model — it’s feasible to be the only result a homeowner sees when they search.

When lead generation is NOT worth it

  • You’re already fully booked through referrals and don’t want more work
  • Your average job value is very low (under $300) and margins are thin
  • You can’t respond to leads within an hour — speed-to-contact is critical for inbound leads
  • You’re evaluating a shared-lead model without running the per-job cost math first

When it clearly is worth it

  • You have available capacity and want consistent monthly volume
  • Your average job is over $1,000
  • You can reliably call back leads within 30–60 minutes
  • The lead source is exclusive and from a site that demonstrably ranks
